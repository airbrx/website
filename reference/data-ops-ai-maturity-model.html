DataOps-AI Capability Maturity Model



This page breaks down the DataOps-AI Capability Maturity Model, a practical framework for assessing and evolving your organization's approach to data orchestration, governance, quality, access, AI readiness, and business alignment. Whether you're just getting started or operating at scale, you'll find clear levels, key capabilities, and outcomes to guide your journey.



Dimension Focus
Data Orchestration & Architecture Workflow management, lineage, infrastructure maturity
Data Governance & Compliance Policies, cataloging, auditability, privacy
Data Quality & Reliability Validation, observability, SLAs, data contracts

ML/AI Operational Readiness Model lifecycle, feature management, ethics & monitoring

Maturity Levels

Level 1: Ad Hoc

  • Data: Siloed scripts, minimal orchestration. No central governance or catalog.
  • AI: Experimentation only. No reproducibility, security, or visibility.
  • Business: Analytics disconnected from outcomes.

Level 2: Reactive

  • Data: Ingestion and transformation processes stabilized using some orchestration (e.g., cron jobs, Lambdas).
  • AI: Initial notebooks/models developed by isolated teams. No production deployment patterns.
  • Business: Insights used manually, KPIs inconsistently tied to data assets.

Level 3: Standardized (Baseline Target for Modern DataOps)

  • Data:
    • Airflow (Astronomer) orchestrates all ingestion and transformations.
    • Databricks Lakehouse manages the bronze â†’ gold flow.
    • Unity Catalog applied across tables for access and lineage.
  • AI:
    • Centralized model development with defined environments.
    • Some features managed centrally (early feature store).
    • Deployment via Databricks MLflow or similar, but limited monitoring.
  • Business:
    • Gold datasets drive operational dashboards, billing, forecasting, etc.
    • Strategic KPIs mapped to curated tables.
    • C4E enables business team adoption of governed pipelines.

Level 4: Proactive

  • Data:
    • Data contracts enforced via DLT expectations.
    • Astronomer Observe monitors DAG health, freshness, and lineage.
    • Metadata and classifications drive downstream use and policy enforcement.
  • AI:
    • Feature store is standardized, versioned, and reused.
    • Model governance processes (review boards, risk assessments) in place.
    • Monitoring of model drift, performance, and explainability integrated.
  • Business:
    • AI is used in production to optimize campaigns, pricing, churn reduction.
    • Business units trust and understand AI outputs via traceable lineage and quality indicators.

Level 5: Strategic & Adaptive

  • Data:
    • DataOps teams operate as internal product teams.
    • Real-time pipelines support low-latency decisioning.
    • Full cost, usage, and impact metrics guide data investments.
  • AI:
    • Continuous delivery of models with retraining, drift response, and ethical oversight.
    • AI usage embedded in operational processes and customer experiences.
  • Business:
    • Executive dashboards fed by real-time gold-layer data tied directly to P&L levers.
    • New revenue streams emerge from AI-powered services/products.
