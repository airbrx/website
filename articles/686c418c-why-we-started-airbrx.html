Why We Started Airbrx

We've Seen This Before

We started Airbrx because we kept seeing the same thing happen. Companies would invest heavily in cloud platforms, orchestration tools, or data infrastructure, but six months or a year later, leaders were still asking:


 “Why isn’t this delivering real results?”

 

It wasn’t because the technology was bad. It was because the focus was off. Too often, projects got stuck optimizing the platform instead of solving the problem.

 

We knew there was a better way — and we had the experience to deliver it.

 

A Team That’s Been Around the Block

Among us — Ben Tallman, Amit Beria, Michael Bissell, and Stephen Shannon — we’ve spent decades building and running technology and operations in complex environments: financial services, professional services, and high-tech SaaS. We've been clients and delivery partners. We've led companies, departments, teams, shipped product, supported customers, and built things that lasted.

 

We’ve also seen what doesn’t work — and how easy it is for smart teams to get bogged down chasing the wrong goals.

 

That’s the perspective we bring to Airbrx.

 

We Don’t Do Hype

We’re not interested in chasing trends for the sake of it. The tools matter — and we know them well (Databricks, Airflow, Snowflake, AWS, GCP, Azure, dbt, and the rest) — but we see them for what they are: tools.

 

What we care about is helping clients make real progress. That means solving actual problems, building things that are maintainable, and keeping complexity in check.

 

Our Approach: Build First Versions, Not Just Demos

One of the biggest traps in data and AI projects is the cycle of proofs-of-concept that never make it into production.

 

We avoid that. We focus on building first versions — real, usable solutions that move the needle. You can iterate on something that works. You can’t iterate on a deck.

 

Why Now?

AI has changed the game. A lot of the mechanics of building software are getting easier. But that just makes experience, context and domain expertise even more valuable.

 

In this environment, the advantage doesn’t go to whoever builds the most — it goes to whoever understands what to build, why, and how it will drive the business forward.

 

That’s where we come in.

 

Who We Work With

A lot of our work is in financial services, SaaS and professional services businesses, but we’ve also led teams and delivered outcomes in a multitude of other areas. Different industries, same challenges: legacy systems, complex processes, unclear paths to value.

 

We help cut through that.
