Data Has Gravity – Let’s Talk About Why That Matters

Thursday, May 29, 2025


 

At Airbrx, we like to keep things grounded—but when it comes to data, that’s not just a metaphor. “Data has gravity” is one of those deceptively simple phrases that packs a punch. And if you’re building anything serious with data—AI, analytics, apps, or operations—you’ve pull against it. Hard.

 

Technically speaking, gravity is a property of mass—and data has mass. Not in the physical sense, but in the way it influences architecture, systems, and decision-making. As your data grows, it gets heavier in every way that matters to your tech stack. That’s the mass. The gravity? That’s everything else getting pulled into orbit around it.

 

Here’s the deal: as your business grows, your data grows. A lot. And all those dashboards, processes, models, and apps? They don’t just live in the cloud—they orbit your data. Try moving it around too much, and things break, get expensive, AND they’re going to slow down. That’s gravity in action.

 

Instead of dragging data to your compute, a modern Lakehouse strategy uses orchestration to bring compute to the data. That’s the magic of the Lakehouse. You don’t fight gravity—you use it to your advantage.

 

So, What’s the Big Deal About Data Gravity?

Data isn’t just a byproduct: it’s the center of your business. And as your business succeeds and grows, the bigger the mass of data gets And then, more everything else – compute, tools, people – gets pulled in. 

 

Here’s how all those things get pulled into the gravity well of your data:

 

  • AI and Analytics: Models run faster and smarter when they sit next to the data they’re trained on.
     
  • Infrastructure: Want to avoid crazy egress fees and latency nightmares? Then don’t move your data if you don’t have to.
     
  • Complexity and Cost: Shifting data creates drag. It creates drag on your systems and your budget. It creates a drag on time. 

     

Enter the Chief Data Officer (CDO)

All this talk about gravity, orbits, and momentum? It’s why we’re big believers in the CDO role. Someone needs to chart the course—decide when to centralize, when to federate, when to mesh, and how to keep your data strategy from spinning out of control. A great CDO keeps your business aligned with your data’s natural gravity and helps you build smart, scalable systems around it.

 

Park Your Compute Where the Data Lives

Stop fighting the gravity. The cloud and platforms like Databricks and Snowflake flipped the script. Instead of moving data around, compute spins up where the data already sits. This Lakehouse model cuts duplication and  latency, simplifies architecture, and respects the natural physics of your data stack.

 

Data Momentum Is Real—Make Sure It’s the Right Kind

Here’s the thing: when you move data, it builds momentum. But data momentum is tricky—it can create chaos, slow you down, and cost a fortune. You want business momentum. That means keeping your data stable and letting your insights, decisions, and customer value fly fast.

 

Don’t Fight Gravity—Design for It

Innovative teams don’t pretend gravity doesn’t exist—they design for it. And some basic things you can do include:

 

  • Build Orbits, Not Silos: Let systems, teams, and tools revolve around shared, trusted data—not their own copies of it.
     
  • Orchestrate Near the Source: Use orchestration platforms like Astronomer Airflow to trigger workflows where the data lives.
     
  • Go Lakehouse, Go Mesh: These architectures let you scale without friction. They make data usable, governed, and powerful—right where it is.
